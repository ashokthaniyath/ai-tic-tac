Tic-Tac-Toe is a simple yet engaging strategy game played between two players. The objective is to be the first to align three of your symbols (either 'X' or 'O') in a row, column, or diagonal on a grid. It's a classic game that has been enjoyed across generations due to its quick playtime and tactical nature.

The rules of Tic-Tac-Toe:

  1. The game is played on a grid, typically 3x3, but can be expanded to larger grids for variation.
  2. Players take turns placing their symbol (either 'X' or 'O') in an empty cell on the grid.
  3. The first player to align three of their symbols in a row, column, or diagonal wins the game.
  4. If all the cells are filled and neither player has achieved a winning alignment, the game ends in a draw.

Variations of Tic-Tac-Toe:
  • Classic Tic-Tac-Toe: Played on a standard 3x3 grid.
  • Larger Grids: Grids like 4x4 or 5x5, where players may need to align more symbols (e.g., 4 in a row).
  • 3D Tic-Tac-Toe: A more complex version played on a 3D grid, requiring alignment across multiple planes.
  • Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe: A game where each cell of a larger grid contains a smaller Tic-Tac-Toe game, and winning the overall grid depends on winning smaller games.

Enjoy playing and honing your strategic thinking skills!
